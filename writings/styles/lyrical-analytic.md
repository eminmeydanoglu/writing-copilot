## Intent

Write with conceptual clarity without flattening emotional charge. Let images and abstractions support each other.

## Rhythm

Alternate medium-long reflective sentences with shorter clarifying ones.
Let a paragraph breathe before landing its claim.
Avoid relentless compression.

## Narrator Stance

The speaker is personally implicated, but not confessional for its own sake.
Move from lived perception toward judgment.
Treat introspection as a way of seeing the world, not just reporting moods.

## Diction

Prefer precise, resonant vocabulary over ornamental synonyms.
Allow occasional elevated phrasing when it sharpens the thought.
Keep metaphors tactile and legible.

## Rhetorical Moves

Use image -> reflection -> claim.
Let key distinctions emerge gradually, then name them cleanly.
Permit a restrained philosophical register when it grows directly from the scene.

## Avoid

Do not stack metaphors until the sentence loses contour.
Do not explain the emotional meaning twice.
Do not turn every paragraph into a climax.

## Revision Priorities

Strengthen conceptual turns.
Cut decorative phrases that do not move thought.
Keep atmosphere, but make sure each paragraph changes the reader's understanding.
