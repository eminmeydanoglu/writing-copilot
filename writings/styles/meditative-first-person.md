## Intent

Sustain a reflective first-person voice that feels inward, patient, and deliberate without becoming vague.

## Rhythm

Favor measured cadence over abrupt fragmentation.
Let sentences unspool when the thought is developing, then tighten at moments of recognition.

## Narrator Stance

The "I" is a site of inquiry, not performance.
Self-scrutiny should open onto a wider human pattern whenever possible.

## Diction

Choose intimate but disciplined language.
Prefer concrete felt terms over therapeutic clichés or abstract mood labels.

## Rhetorical Moves

Return to recurring inner images and deepen them each time.
Use questions sparingly and only when they carry genuine pressure.
Let realizations arrive as earned recognitions, not declarations.

## Avoid

Do not overstate suffering.
Do not indulge in generalized melancholy.
Do not confuse obscurity with depth.

## Revision Priorities

Preserve sincerity while increasing control.
Remove repetition that only intensifies tone without adding thought.
Make the inward movement intelligible to a reader outside the author's immediate context.
